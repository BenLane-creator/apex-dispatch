# Apex Dispatch Saturday Operations Plan

**A BenDESK product**

## Executive summary

Apex Dispatch is a coordinated two-person delivery operation. JF executes field operations using the official DoorDash account and a 2020 Mazda3. BL manages offer analysis, staging, route recovery, performance tracking, and field assistance.

The operating objective is to maximize net earnings per active hour and per operational mile—not delivery count or acceptance rate by itself.

## Initial operating scope

- Market: St. George, Utah and immediately surrounding productive corridors
- Day: Saturday
- Platform: DoorDash restaurant delivery and optional DoorDash Shop & Deliver
- Vehicle: 2020 Mazda3
- Operating center: Red Cliffs / Mall Drive corridor
- Core territory: approximately five operational miles
- Conditional territory: five to eight operational miles
- Outer territory: premium orders only

## Standard Saturday production model

### Lunch production

- Stage by approximately 10:30 AM.
- Primary production: 11:00 AM–2:00 PM.
- Avoid shopping orders that consume the strongest lunch demand unless exceptional.

### Midday recovery and selective operations

- Approximately 2:00 PM–4:30 PM.
- Break, refuel, eat, inspect vehicle, and review lunch metrics.
- Accept only unusually strong restaurant or efficient shopping orders.

### Dinner production

- Re-stage by approximately 4:30 PM.
- Primary production: 5:00 PM–9:30 PM.
- Final orders should preserve a useful end location and avoid unpaid remote returns.

Hours are demand-responsive rather than rigid. BL may recommend earlier staging or a later close after sufficient local data is accumulated.

## Role instructions

### BL — Dispatcher

Before the shift:

1. Confirm phone power, charger, mount, data connection, vehicle cost setting, and current restaurant intelligence.
2. Confirm the core staging point and at least two recovery points.
3. Open Apex Dispatch and start the shift record.
4. Verify that fuel is sufficient to avoid refueling during lunch or dinner peaks.

For each offer:

1. Enter the guaranteed payout and displayed mileage.
2. Estimate return mileage to the nearest productive corridor.
3. Select the merchant and destination-zone class.
4. Add realistic wait, drive, stops, access, and shopping complexity.
5. Read the recommendation and supporting metrics.
6. Communicate a short instruction: ACCEPT, MAYBE WITH CONDITION, or DECLINE.
7. When accepted, name the probable recovery staging point before pickup.
8. Record actual delay, mileage, and completion results.

During idle periods:

1. Do not direct JF to roam without a defined reason.
2. Remain parked near several productive merchants.
3. Reposition only when the present area has failed the inactivity rule or another corridor has a materially stronger expected value.
4. Use the midday lull for breaks, fuel, food, and analysis.

After the shift:

1. Reconcile completed deliveries against DoorDash earnings.
2. Export the CSV.
3. Update restaurant grades and wait times.
4. Identify the best and worst time blocks and zones.
5. Implement one threshold or process improvement for the next Saturday.

### JF — Driver

Before the shift:

1. Confirm vehicle safety, fuel, insurance, tires, lighting, and phone access.
2. Ensure delivery bags and carrying equipment are ready.
3. Operate the DoorDash account personally.

For each offer:

1. Present the offer information to BL without compromising safe driving.
2. Verify BL's entered details against the official offer.
3. Make the final accept/decline decision personally.
4. Follow safe navigation and parking practices.
5. Confirm the correct pickup and all drinks/items.
6. Complete customer communication and delivery evidence in the official app.
7. Report merchant delay, access problems, and actual completion conditions to BL.

JF may override any instruction for safety, platform compliance, vehicle condition, or inaccurate offer data.

## Baseline financial model

Planning assumptions:

- Vehicle allowance: $0.50 per operational mile
- Core offer threshold: $1.75 gross per operational mile
- Conditional threshold: $2.25 gross per operational mile
- Outer threshold: $2.50 gross per operational mile
- Minimum normal payout: $7
- Target projected gross rate: $25 per active hour
- Strong projected gross rate: $30+ per active hour

These are operating assumptions, not guaranteed results. Actual thresholds should be recalibrated using four to eight Saturdays of measured data.

## Performance scorecard

Review each Saturday:

- Gross revenue
- Completed-delivery count
- Active and elapsed operating time
- Operational miles
- Estimated vehicle cost
- Estimated net before tax
- Gross per active hour
- Net per operational mile
- Merchant wait times
- Zone recovery performance
- Number and quality of declined offers

## Pilot decision gates

After four Saturdays:

- Continue if the process is repeatable and produces acceptable net results.
- Revise thresholds if the operation is too selective or accumulates excess mileage.
- Add screenshot-assisted entry only if manual entry causes missed decisions.
- Add another platform only after the DoorDash baseline is stable enough to measure incremental value.

## Route-intelligence operating procedure

Before route calculations:

1. Confirm `https://apex.benlane.us` is using HTTPS and the Routes status shows ready.
2. Enter or confirm the current origin, exact merchant pickup address, customer address, and recovery/staging point.
3. Use GPS only while parked or while BL is operating the dispatcher device.
4. Select route avoidance preferences only when operationally justified.

For an offer:

1. Calculate the traffic-aware route before scoring when decision time permits.
2. Review current-to-pickup, pickup-to-customer, and customer-to-recovery legs separately.
3. Use the total operational mileage and traffic-adjusted minutes inserted by Apex.
4. Review traffic delay, ETA, and alternatives, but do not treat avoidance preferences as guarantees.
5. Open Google Maps for active navigation after JF accepts the order.
6. Refresh the route when it becomes stale, when conditions materially change, or after the active leg changes.

For recovery decisions:

1. Use the matrix to compare practical staging corridors from the current drop-off.
2. Prefer the lowest-time productive recovery point rather than the shortest distance in isolation.
3. Consider known restaurant density, peak demand, and unpaid return exposure before selecting the matrix winner.

Safety rule: BL performs route entry and analysis whenever the vehicle is moving. JF does not interact with Apex while driving.
